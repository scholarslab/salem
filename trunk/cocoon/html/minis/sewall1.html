Samuel Sewall

Born in England in 1652, Samuel Sewall moved to America as a child and obtained two degrees in theology before marring into a wealthy Boston family. As a result, Sewall never became a minister but rather a wealthy merchant with public stature. He was appointed to sit as a judge for the witchcraft trials in the Court of Oyer and Terminer, and he kept a richly detailed daily diary that has helped scholars piece together the trials. Sewall's public confession of sin on the Day of Prayer and Fasting (January 14, 1697) five years after the trials, demonstrate Sewall's remorse for his actions in condemning innocent people.
