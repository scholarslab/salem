Rev. Samuel Parris

Samuel Parris was born in London in 1654. In 1680, Parris returned from Barbados to Boston and worked as a merchant until taking up the position of minister in the conflict ridden Salem Village. Parris became involved in the Salem Witchcraft Trials because of two children in his family and his own overbearing attempts to control the members of his church. From his pulpit he preached about the Devil and the threats on the village from the Devil's presence. Within his family, his daughter and live-in niece were among the first to become saccusers. Parris himself appears in over 22 court documents, both as a court recorder and an accusing voice. He interpreted opposition to his sermons and efforts at control as the Devil's work. He finally fell to the pressures of his opponents in the village and left the pulpit in 1696. He then went on to hold various occupations, including that of a minister, teacher, land retailer in several different communities. He died in Sudbury in 1720.
